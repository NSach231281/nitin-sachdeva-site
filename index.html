About

About & decision philosophy

I work at the intersection of AI, optimisation and decision science – explicitly modelling uncertainty, constraints and trade-offs for leadership-level decisions.

My journey spans seven years of teaching and research in leading business schools and over a decade of building decision systems in industry. At TVS Motor, I have led high-impact work in demand forecasting, inventory optimisation, marketing mix modelling and dealer networks. Earlier, at Protiviti, I worked on COVID-19 forecasting, HR and logistics optimisation, and fraud analytics.

I am usually brought in when decisions cut across silos, when intuition and dashboards disagree, and when the downside of getting it wrong is no longer acceptable.

Decision principles

  • Decisions matter more than predictions.
  • Constraints define reality; they cannot be an afterthought.
  • Uncertainty must be explicit, not buried in point estimates.
  • Models must survive governance, not just validation metrics.
  • Systems should make trade-offs visible, not hide them in tuning.

Domains

Where decision science creates leverage

I focus on environments where decisions span multiple time horizons and stakeholders, and where the cost of naive optimisation or local analytics is material.

Enterprise decision systems

When decisions span supply chain, commercial and finance; when local KPIs fight each other; and when no one owns the full decision loop. Architecture and governance matter as much as algorithms.

Hybrid ML + optimisation

When good predictions are not enough – pricing, inventory, scheduling, routing, marketing allocation – and constraints, scenarios and risk must be explicit in the decision logic.

Operations research & planning

When combinatorial complexity and inter-dependencies defeat heuristics: multi-echelon inventory, allocation, network design, plant–AWH–dealer flows, capacity and workforce planning.

Statistical modelling & risk

When leadership needs ranges, scenarios and downside protection – not a single number – for demand, risk, campaign response or pandemic curves.

Selected work

Flagship decision systems

A small sample of systems I have designed or led where decisions were high-stakes, uncertainty was real and outcomes mattered.

Manufacturing · Forecasting & MEIO

Demand forecasting + end-to-end inventory optimisation (TVS)

Integrated demand forecasting, inventory policy design and multi-echelon optimisation across plants, AWHs and 1,300+ dealers – making uncertainty and service trade-offs explicit.
→ Enabled system-level optimisation of service, working capital and replenishment under demand volatility.
Manufacturing · Marketing decisions

Bayesian MMM + MTA decision framework (TVS)

Built a joint MMM + MTA system to separate portfolio-level budget moves from within-channel decisions, under limited experimentation and noisy signals.
→ Allowed leadership to reallocate spend with controlled downside risk and coherent macro counterfactuals.
Automotive · Process optimisation

Paint transfer efficiency – hybrid ML + OR + DOE

Combined design of experiments, machine learning and optimisation to improve paint transfer efficiency under real shop-floor constraints.
→ Significant annual material cost savings with stable quality performance.
Healthcare · Centre effectiveness

Healthcare centre effectiveness using DEA

Built a DEA-based framework to benchmark centres on efficiency and effectiveness across throughput, resource utilisation and service outcomes.
→ Helped leadership identify structurally under-performing centres and prioritise interventions.
Healthcare · Patient flow

Patient waiting time reduction using queuing & simulation

Used queuing theory and AnyLogic simulation to re-design outpatient flow, staffing and appointment rules across multiple scenarios.
→ Reduced waiting times and congestion without proportional staffing increases.
Public health · Pandemic forecasting

COVID-19 forecasting & scenario modelling

Co-developed forecasting and scenario systems during early COVID-19 waves, combining epidemiological and time-series models to inform communication and planning.
→ Provided decision ranges in a period of extreme uncertainty, used by media and institutional stakeholders.
Telecom · Fraud analytics

Wangiri (single-ring) fraud detection system

Built near real-time fraud detection on call-detail records, combining probabilistic scoring and ML to prioritise alerts and reduce false positives.
→ Shortened detection cycles and materially improved fraud response efficiency.
