What is Enterprise Decision Science?

The discipline of turning information into better actions at enterprise scale, under uncertainty and constraints.

For me, decision science is not about chasing perfect models. It is about designing systems where uncertainty is explicit, trade-offs are transparent, and decisions are made in a way that leadership can understand, govern, and trust — even when conditions change.

About

About & Decision Philosophy

I work at the intersection of AI, optimization, and decision science, focusing on problems where scale, uncertainty, and constraints make intuition and isolated analytics unreliable.

My background spans academic research (PhD in Operations Research, 40+ publications) and deep industry application in manufacturing, healthcare, retail, and financial services — including leadership roles shaping enterprise decision systems.

I am typically engaged when organizations face decisions that cut across functions, involve explicit trade-offs, and carry material business or public impact.

Decision Philosophy

  • Decisions matter more than predictions.
  • Constraints define reality — they cannot be an afterthought.
  • Uncertainty must be explicit, not hidden in single-number outputs.
  • Models must survive governance, not just validation metrics.
  • Systems should make trade-offs visible, not bury them in tuning and dashboards.
Domains

Decision Science Domains

I am usually called when traditional analytics and dashboards are no longer enough — and when decisions span multiple time horizons, constraints, and stakeholders.

Enterprise Decision Systems

Required when decisions span multiple functions, time horizons, and constraints — and when local optimization or isolated analytics begins to break the system. Signals include trade-offs between cost, service, and risk, cascading decisions across silos, and unclear accountability for outcomes.

Hybrid ML + Optimization Systems

Required when prediction alone is insufficient — and when decisions must be made under constraints, uncertainty, and competing objectives. Signals include models that perform on paper but fail to improve outcomes, or parameter settings that cannot be tuned manually at scale.

Operations Research & Mathematical Modeling

Required when problems involve combinatorial complexity, interdependent choices, and system-level trade-offs that intuition and heuristics cannot reliably resolve — such as scheduling, allocation, routing, and multi-stage planning under constraints.

Statistical Modeling & Uncertainty Quantification

Required when uncertainty materially affects decisions and point estimates create false confidence. Critical where leadership decisions depend on confidence intervals, risk bounds, and downside protection rather than a single forecasted number.

Selected Work

Selected Decision Systems

A small sample of systems I have designed or led, where decisions were high-stakes, uncertainty was real, and outcomes mattered.

Manufacturing · Forecasting & MEIO

Demand Forecasting + End-to-End Inventory Optimization (TVS)

Designed an end-to-end decision system integrating demand forecasting, inventory policy design, and multi-echelon optimization across plants, warehouses, and dealer networks.
→ Enabled system-level optimization of service, working capital, and replenishment under demand uncertainty.
Manufacturing · Marketing Investment Decisions

Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) + MTA Decision Framework — TVS

Built an integrated MMM + MTA framework to guide large-scale marketing budget allocations under limited experimentation, distinguishing portfolio-level and within-channel decisions with Bayesian MMM and MTA.
→ Enabled leadership to reallocate spend with controlled downside risk and sustained volume stability, without fragile causal claims.
Automotive · Process Optimization

Paint Transfer Efficiency Optimization — Hybrid ML + OR + DOE

Designed a hybrid system combining Design of Experiments, machine learning, and optimization to improve paint transfer efficiency under real shop-floor constraints.
→ Delivered significant annual material cost savings with stable quality outcomes.
Healthcare · Center Effectiveness

Healthcare Center Effectiveness using DEA

Built a Data Envelopment Analysis framework to assess relative efficiency and effectiveness of healthcare centers across throughput, resource utilization, and service outcomes.
→ Enabled leadership to identify structurally underperforming centers and prioritize targeted interventions.
Healthcare · Patient Flow & Experience

Patient Waiting Time Reduction using Queuing & Simulation

Developed a queuing and simulation model (AnyLogic) to analyze patient flow, bottlenecks, and service time variability across outpatient processes, testing alternate staffing and scheduling policies.
→ Reduced waiting times and congestion without proportional staffing increases, improving throughput and experience.
Public Health · Pandemic Forecasting

COVID-19 Pandemic Forecasting & Scenario Modeling

Co-designed a forecasting and scenario-analysis system during early COVID-19 waves using epidemiological and time-series modeling to project trajectories under different interventions.
→ Projections used for public communication and stakeholder briefings during a period of extreme uncertainty.
Telecom · Fraud Analytics

Telecom Fraud Detection — Wangiri (Single-Ring) Fraud

Built a near real-time fraud detection system on call detail records, combining probabilistic scoring and ML to prioritize alerts and reduce false positives.
→ Reduced detection cycle time and analyst effort with materially improved fraud response.
